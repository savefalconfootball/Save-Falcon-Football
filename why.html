This is not working


In every way measurable, Mike Jinks is not working as head coach at Bowling Green. He began as head coach in 2016 after a run of unprecedented success in Falcon football.
The previous four years before Jinks began encompassed two head coaches. Those four years included four bowl games, three MAC East championships, and two MAC championships.
As soon as Mike Jinks took over, the program fell apart and has not improved. Under Mike Jinks' guidance, the team has vastly underperformed.
  • The worst two losses in modern Bowling Green football history
  • A continuously underpeforming defense, even with changing defensive coordinators
  • The nation's worst run defense
  • Bottom of the MAC in nearly every defensive category for three years
  • Seven total wins so far in three years
  • Two close wins over FCS teams
  • First loss to an FCS team in 30 years
  • No non-conference FBS wins in three years
  • No wins over bowl teams, so far in three years
  • No wins over MAC teams playing their starting QB, so far in three years
  • Bottom of the nation in ESPN's "Fan Happiness Index"
  • Led the 2017-2018 offseason nationwide in most arrests nationwide
  • High attrition levels, which offset recruiting efforts

On the field, the Bowling Green football program has sunk to levels unimaginable after the successes prior to Jinks' arrival.
As the final points above indicate, the problems with Bowling Green football under Mike Jinks extend to off the field as well.
This is not working. For the good of the future of Bowling Green football, it is time to replace Mike Jinks as Bowling Green Football's head coach.
We wish him the best in his career and hope he has future successes.
